Module 5: Crop Selection and Calendar Planning

Urban agriculture in Baguio City is more than just farming; it’s about transforming small spaces into lush, thriving green corners that feed communities, nourish the earth, and bring people closer together. With careful crop selection and clever calendar planning, you can unlock the full potential of Baguio’s unique climate to create a sustainable and rewarding urban farming experience.

1. Factors That Shape Crop Selection

  • Climate and Weather: Baguio’s cool, temperate climate is a dream for crops like lettuce, spinach, and carrots. Meanwhile, the rainy season calls for resilient crops like beans or leafy greens. And for the sunny rooftops? Heat-loving crops like okra and eggplant soak it all up!
    Example Crops: Leafy greens (lettuce, kale), root crops (carrots, radishes), and heat-tolerant options (okra, basil).
  • Maximizing Space: Think vertically! Climbing crops like beans, cucumbers, and tomatoes love trellises, while herbs and leafy vegetables thrive in compact containers or window boxes.
  • Market Demand: Baguio’s thriving food scene craves fresh, local produce. From aromatic herbs like basil to crisp lettuce and vibrant tomatoes, there’s always a market for flavorful, nutrient-rich crops.

2. Seasonal Crop Calendars: Your Year-Round Guide

A well-planned crop calendar doesn’t just ensure bountiful harvests; it keeps your soil healthy, reduces pests, and maximizes your growing potential throughout the year. Here’s how to plan for success:

  • Cool Season Crops (November–February): Perfect for Baguio’s dry, cool months. Focus on leafy greens (lettuce, spinach), root crops (carrots, radishes), and cool-weather favorites like broccoli and cauliflower.
  • Warm Season Crops (March–June): Embrace the warmth with tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, and sun-loving herbs like basil and oregano.
  • Rainy Season Crops (July–October): Resilient crops like beans, peas, and sweet potatoes shine in wetter conditions, ensuring your garden thrives even through the rain.

Pro Tips for Thriving Across Seasons

  • Use Greenhouses and Shade Nets: Protect crops from unpredictable weather while extending your growing season.
  • Go Vertical with Raised Beds: Maximize drainage during the rainy season and save space for high-yield crops like strawberries and peppers.
  • Master Water Management: Use drip irrigation to deliver consistent water to plants while avoiding waterlogging during heavy rains.

By tailoring your crop choices and planning around Baguio’s unique seasons, you can cultivate a garden that thrives year-round. Whether you’re growing broccoli in November or basil in March, every season brings fresh opportunities to grow, share, and enjoy the fruits of your labor. Let’s make every square inch of Baguio’s urban spaces bloom with life!
